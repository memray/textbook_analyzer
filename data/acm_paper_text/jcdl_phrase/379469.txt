digital-library collaborations in a world community digital libraries and their user-communities are increasingly internat ional in nature . however - though technological progress and global education have brought american and european communities closer - cross-cultural and other crosscutting issues impede the formation of & lquo ; world community & rquo ; on larger scales . the pertinent issues include : collaboration in the presence of language and cultural-barriers , international copyrights , international revenue streams , and universal-access . this panel will examine notions of community from a variety of theoretical and practical perspectives , and discuss lessons that can be gleaned from applications of the community concept . topics are expected to include scalability , sustainability , regenerative cycles in healthy communities , and examples of digital-library efforts that have international potential or implications .
